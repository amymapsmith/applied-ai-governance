The Approach

AI Governance: applied.

Like applied science or applied research, Applied AI Governance is about closing the gap between principle and practice. Most organizations already know responsible AI matters. The harder question is how to make it real (inside the teams, tools, and processes that actually ship AI products).

That's where I come in. Research and regulation are foundational — but applied AI governance is about translating them into real-world practices that actually take hold inside organizations.

Services

How I can help

AI Use Case Review Design

Build or streamline the process your organization uses to evaluate new AI use cases, from initial intake through responsible deployment. Clear roles, documentation, and decision criteria that actually get used.

Responsible AI Roadmapping

Turn a collection of policy commitments into a prioritized, executable program. I help teams identify gaps, sequence initiatives, and build accountability structures that hold up in practice.

LLM & GenAI Onboarding

Establish repeatable processes for bringing new AI vendors, tools, and models into your organization, coordinating legal, security, and engineering from the start.

Cross-Functional Alignment

Responsible AI touches every function. I help legal, data science, product, and policy teams build shared language and shared processes, so governance isn't siloed in a single team.

Program Audits & Assessments

An honest look at where your AI governance program is and where it needs to go. Practical recommendations grounded in what high-functioning programs actually look like inside real organizations.

Advising & Office Hours

Ongoing strategic advising for leaders building or maturing an AI governance function. Think of it as a trusted thought partner who has built this from the inside.

About

I've built this from the inside.

10+ Years working with data, AI, and policy

I'm Amy Smith, a data scientist turned Technical Program Manager with a track record of turning ambitious, ambiguous problems into structured, scalable programs. My career has spanned GIS analysis, data science, policy research, and most recently Responsible AI, always at the intersection of technical depth and organizational complexity.

At Intuit, I served as a Staff TPM for Responsible AI, where I rebuilt the GenAI use case review process, cut LLM onboarding time by 66%, and led a portfolio of responsible AI initiatives across legal, security, data science, and product teams. I represented Intuit at Partnership on AI workshops and forums, bringing an applied, practitioner's perspective to industry-wide conversations.

Most recently, at Plaid, I serve as Senior Staff TPM driving the company's AI Governance strategy across Engineering, Product, Legal, Security, Compliance, and GTM. My work spans everything from internal pilots to AI-powered product features, with a particular focus on responsible, ethical, and transparent AI adoption in FinTech.

What I bring to this work isn't just a framework: it's the lived experience of making governance real inside large, fast-moving tech organizations. I know what breaks down, what gets buy-in, and what actually sticks.
